Investigation into voice protection preserving human perception by speech generation

Authors: XXX
Abstract:This paper focuses on a proactive voice protection method using speech generation, specifically employing VC to modify inherent speaker attributes during speech generation. The disentanglement of speaker and non-speaker attributes, coupled with the fast gradient step method (FGSM) for adversarial attack on the speaker embedding, results in speaker-modified speech utterances. Experiments carried out on the LibriSpeech dataset demonstrate that while some perturbed utterances maintain human-perceptible speaker attributes, automatic speaker verification evaluations reveal higher equal error rates compared to original and non-perturbed utterances. These findings preliminarily affirm the effectiveness of the speech generation approach in protecting speaker information from accurate perception by speaker recognition models without altering human perception.



Here, we showcase three kinds of speech utterances: (1)the original speech; (2)the regenerated(regen) speech that the YourTTS model generated without modifying the speaker embedding vector; (3)the protected(prote) speech generated by adding perturbation to speaker embedding.
